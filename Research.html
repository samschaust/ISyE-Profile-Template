John Doe's Research

Research Interests

I believe that application-motivated theory development is fundamentally important and has real impacts—this is what continuously inspires my research. In particular, I am fascinated by challenging optimization problems such as scheduling, routing, matching, and many others, where system uncertainties and dynamics need to be considered while making large-scale complex decisions. They often involve integer decision variables and nonlinear constraints embedded in sequential decision-making processes.


In short, my research interests include stochastic programming, integer programming, nonlinear programming and applications of optimization techniques and statistical analysis in service systems.
